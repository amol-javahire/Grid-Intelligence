Here's the complete map of ERCOT tables, straight from the official Public Data API:

Hourly Load / Demand Data
By zone (easiest, available now via API):
ReportNameGranularityNP6-345-CDActual System Load by Weather ZoneHourly, 8 zonesNP6-346-CDActual System Load by Forecast ZoneHourly, 4 zones (North/South/West/Houston)
These are the cleanest — published next-day, go back 7+ years, directly queryable from api.ercot.com.
By electrical bus (harder — ERCOT doesn't publish bus-level load directly):
The proxy is to combine two reports:

NP4-159-CD — "Load Distribution Factors" (each bus's participation factor in its load zone)
× NP6-346-CD zone totals → gives estimated bus-level load

If you want prices at each bus (which is the signal more useful for siting anyway): NP6-787-CD — "LMPs by Electrical Bus" — SCED 5-minute prices at every bus on the ERCOT network. This is what most production cost models use as the locational demand signal.
Historical archive (CSV bulk download, no API auth needed):

ercot.com/gridinfo/load/load_hist — hourly load by weather zone going back to 2002. Easy starting point.

NP3-965-ER is the key one for your use case — it's what Grid Status uses and what academic researchers use for unit-level dispatch analysis. The tradeoff is the 60-day delay (changed to RTC+B schema after Dec 5, 2025). It contains:

Resource name + fuel type
telemetered_net_output (actual MW) every 5 minutes
base_point (what ERCOT instructed)
High/Low sustainable limits
Offer curve segments

For near-real-time generation by fuel type (no delay), the EIA-930 Hourly Electric Grid Monitor publishes ERCOT data with ~1 hour lag, broken down by fuel — though that's aggregated, not unit-level.

What ERCOT Doesn't Publish Publicly

Bus-level load directly (inferred via distribution factors)
Unit-level output with <60-day delay (competitive market data)
Plant-level fuel burn or heat rate actuals

For the app, NP3-965-ER + NP6-787-CD together would give you generation dispatch by unit (historical) + bus-level LMP prices — enough to build a congestion/curtailment attribution model by node.
